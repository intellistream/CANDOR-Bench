\section{Conclusion}
This paper presented StreamSeed, a pluginized optimization module for dynamic ANNS under streaming updates.
By introducing Query Hint Reuse (QHR) with Adaptive Query Hint Control (AQHC), StreamSeed unifies hint construction, online usage control, and state updates within a single policy loop.
The proposed design addresses the stale-hint challenge in dynamic workloads and improves amortized end-to-end efficiency while preserving recall safety through controlled fallback.

Implemented in CANDOR-Bench, StreamSeed enables runbook-driven and backend-comparable evaluation within a reproducible workflow.
Empirical results and ablations indicate that unified hint control is a key ingredient for robust performance under high update rates and distribution drift.

Future work includes deeper backend co-design for dynamic HNSW internals, stronger drift-aware policy learning, and broader cross-dataset generalization studies.
